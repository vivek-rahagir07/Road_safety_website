üö¶ Essential Road Safety Instructions

Basic information for drivers and pedestrians to ensure safety and prevent accidents. Always follow local laws and traffic regulations.

1. Four-Wheeler (Car) Specific Rules

  • Vehicle Maintenance: Regularly check your tires, brakes, fluid levels, and lights. A well-maintained vehicle is critical for safe driving.
  • Adjust and Position: Before starting, adjust your seat, mirrors (rear and side), and head restraints for optimal visibility and comfort.
  • Child Safety Seats: Ensure all children are secured in appropriate child safety seats or booster seats in the back seat, as required by law.
  • Secure Loose Items: Keep heavy or loose items in the trunk or secured in the cabin, as they can become dangerous projectiles in a sudden stop or collision.

2. General Driving Rules (For Motorists)

  • Obey Speed Limits: Never exceed the posted speed limit. Reduce speed in bad weather (rain, fog, snow) or high-traffic areas.
  • Always Wear Your Seat Belt: Ensure all occupants of the vehicle (front and back) are buckled up. It is the most effective safety device.
  • Avoid Distracted Driving: Do not use a mobile phone for texting, calling, or browsing while the vehicle is in motion. Keep your focus entirely on the road.
  • Maintain Following Distance: Use the 3-second rule to ensure a safe gap between your vehicle and the one ahead. Increase to 4 or more seconds in poor conditions.
  • Never Drive Impaired: Driving under the influence of alcohol or drugs is illegal and life-threatening. Plan for a designated driver or use a ride-share service.
  • Use Turn Signals: Signal your intentions (turning or changing lanes) well in advance so other drivers and pedestrians know your plan.

3. Pedestrian and Cyclist Safety

  • Use Crosswalks: Only cross the road at designated crosswalks (zebra crossings) or intersections.
  • Stop, Look, and Listen: Before crossing, Stop at the curb, Look left, right, and left again, and Listen for approaching vehicles.
  • Walk on Sidewalks: Always use the sidewalk. If there is no sidewalk, walk on the side of the road facing oncoming traffic.
  • Be Visible at Night: Wear light-colored clothing or use reflective gear and flashlights when walking after dark.
  • No Distractions: Do not use headphones at a high volume or look at your phone while crossing streets. You need to be aware of your surroundings.

4. Traffic Signals and Their Meaning

  1. Red Light: You must stop completely behind the white stop line.
  2. Yellow Light (Amber): Indicates the light is about to turn red. You must prepare to stop unless you are so close to the intersection that stopping safely would cause a risk.
  3. Green Light: You may proceed through the intersection, but only if the way is clear and safe.
  4. Flashing Red Light: Treat it as a Stop Sign. Stop completely, then proceed only when safe.

5. Common Road Signs and Symbols

Sign Type (Shape/Color) Purpose (What it means) Example Symbol/Instruction
Regulatory Signs (Circles / Red & White) Convey laws and regulations. You must obey these signs. STOP (Octagon), Yield (Triangle), No U-Turn (Red circle with a line through it)
Warning Signs (Triangles or Diamonds / Yellow/Orange) Alert drivers to potential hazards or unusual conditions ahead. Winding Road, Sharp Curve, School Zone (Often a pentagon), Pedestrian Crossing
Information Signs (Rectangles / Blue, Green, or White) Provide guidance, directions, and essential information. Exit Sign, Highway Route Marker, Parking Area

Reminder: Road safety is a shared responsibility. Be patient, be visible, and be predictable!
